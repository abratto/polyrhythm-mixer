Welcome to Alan's Polyrhythm Mixer
Layer two competing meters into a groove — tap in patterns, add voices, and shape the sound.
Pick a polyrhythm. Choose Meter A and Meter B (try 3 against 4), set a tempo, and press Enable Audio. A default pulse is pre-tapped so you hear the groove right away.
Tap steps and add voices. Click boxes in any lane to toggle hits. + Voice layers extra rhythms in the Master, A Phrase, or B Phrase lanes. Nudge shifts one voice; Nudge Group shifts the whole lane.
Extend with phrase lengths. Set A, B, or Master Phrase Length to 2–8 cycles for longer repeating patterns. When a lane has multiple cycles, use Cycle ◀ / ▶ to browse — the label shows AUTO (follows the playhead) or PIN (manual view).
Mix and share. In the Sound Mixer, swap instruments, solo, mute, and adjust volume per channel. Save stores rhythms locally; Share copies a link; Reset Mixer restores defaults.

Polyrhythm Visualization

Three Gears. Three meshing gears as a physical analogy: the center Master Wheel rotates once per measure; each side gear meshes and spins a whole-number ratio faster. In 3-against-4, the master has 12 teeth (LCM of 3 and 4) — one side gear spins 3× and the other 4× per master rotation, realigning at the top to complete the cycle. But while the smaller gears spin faster, each full rotation defines a pulse that is slower than the master's per-tooth pulse — one meter divides the measure into 3 equal pulses (4× slower), the other into 4 equal pulses (3× slower). Their rates sit in a 3:4 ratio — one is 3/4 the rate of the other. Playing both divisions of the same measure at those different rates is the polyrhythm. Orange dots show your tapped steps.

Master Cycle Timeline. One master cycle, left to right. The white playhead sweeps as it plays. Markers per lane: orange dots (master), pink diamonds (A cycle), cyan diamonds (B cycle), red triangles (A phrase), cyan triangles (B phrase) — one row per voice.

Full Pattern Timeline. The entire repeating pattern across all cycles with C1, C2 dividers — one labeled row per voice showing every trigger over the full loop.
